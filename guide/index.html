01 What it is

A company that happens to be
staffed entirely by AI.

Not a chatbot and not a doc generator. InnovArise is an agentic venture studio: a team of 20 AI specialists that takes an idea and runs it through the five-stage venture-build pipeline until an advantaged, commercially viable company comes out the other end. The whole playbook is encoded — so the studio behaves like Alloy & Grupo AG would, every time.

The ideaCheap, plentiful ideas in — advantaged ventures outA fat funnel of 100+ ideas a year is narrowed to ~20 Discover, 2–3 Incubate, and one launched company. Killing is cheap and unemotional.
The substrateA repo per ventureEach company is its own self-contained, queryable record of every assumption tested and every dollar spent.
Who runs it
Run like a company — a Board and a CEO.
Board / ICYou (the human team). Approve themes, budgets, launches; gather the real-world evidence AI can't; make the final call.
Studio CEOThe Studio Director agent. Runs the whole AI org, proposes ventures, makes & documents gate decisions, reports every morning.
The org18 specialist agents the CEO orchestrates — builders, researchers, the adversarial VC, the Bar Raiser, and the integrity functions.

04 How it gets better over time

The studio runs a kaizen loop every morning.

Before the daily board update, the studio learns from how the humans edited its outputs and how the Board talked about the work, then upgrades its own agents, skills, and methodology. Small fixes are applied and logged; bigger changes are proposed for approval. It compounds — the studio you use next month is sharper than today's.

Learns from your edits
Every time you rewrite a memo or correct a call, the /studio-improve loop infers the rule and bakes it in.
Source Steward keeps it current
/source-review finds newer, better data, re-verifies every citation, and cascades the update across all docs.
The Board can hire new agents
When a capability gap recurs, the CEO proposes a new AI employee; you approve it into the org. (Three were hired this way already.)
